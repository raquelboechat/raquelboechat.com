The writer and “Senhorita Carioca”

For Antonio Torres, “Senhorita Carioca has not only the merits of being well-researched, but also has a light touch to the text, starting with the title itself, but also is easy to read, and thus brings the average reader that bit closer to the history of Rio de Janeiro” and concluded that “for such a reader, this is a book that will certainly contribute to raising interest for other books that have been or will be written.”

By coincidence, at the same period as Antonio Torres was doing research for “O Nobre Seqüestrador” (The Noble Kidnapper), a novel about the French Corsair Duguay-Trouin, who, during the reign of Louis XIV robbed and killed in the name of the King, an early version of “Senhorita Carioca” was covering one of the most curious and violent episodes of the City’s history, the holding to ransom of Rio de Janeiro. The captor: Duguay-Trouin.

It was this common interest in the same story and the history of Rio that brought the two writers together.

While sitting in a café in the Ipanema district of Rio Antonio Torres, Raquel Boechat and Tomil Gonçalves got talking about their projects about the city during the first centuries of colonization, sketched the most likely routes taken by Trouin in his trek around the hills of Rio and spent several entertaining hours in an imaginary voyage behind the scenes of history.

Antonio Torres

One of the most successful Brazilian authors, journalist and advertising professionals.

His first book was published in 1972 and he now has 13 novels in print, with translations in Cuba, Argentina, France, Germany, Italy, Britain the USA, Israel and the Netherlands.

A prize-winning writer in several countries, Antonio Torres  was made a member by the French Government of the prestigious order of merit  “Chevalier des Arts et des Lettres”, in 1988, for his novels The Land  “Essa Terra” and “Um Táxi para Viena d’Áustria” (A Taxi to Vienna, Austria). He received the Machado de Assis prize from the Brazilian Academy of Letters for his life’s work and won the Passo Fundo Zaffari & Bourbon prize for literature in 2001 for “My Dear Cannibal”.
