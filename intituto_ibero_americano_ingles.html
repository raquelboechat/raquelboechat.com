Germany and “Senhorita Carioca”

The project was submitted to the Ibero-American Institute, Prussian Cultural Heritage Foundation in Berlin in September 2005, through Publications Director Dr. Peter Birle, the Institute offered institutional support to the “Senhorita Carioca” project both because of the merits of its contents and the subject of the research.

At the time we were provided with contacts and procedures in order for us to arrange to have the book published in Germany at a future date.

The project came to the attention of researcher and professor of Latin-American and Portuguese-language literature at the University of Berlin, Berthold Zilly, who is famous for his translation into German of the Brazilian classic “Os Sertões” (translated into English as “Rebellion in the Backlands”) by Euclides da Cunha. Berthold Zilly learned a little more about the form and content of the book in Berlin, and later in both Rio de Janeiro and São Paulo.

We would like to take this opportunity to thank Mr. Zilly, Dr. Birle and the Ibero-American Institute for being among the most generous backers of the Project in Europe.

The Ibero-American Institute

The Ibero-American Institute, Prussian Cultural Heritage Foundation, is a centre of interdisciplinary scientific and cultural exchange focused on Latin America, Spain, and Portugal. The Institute houses Europe‘s largest library specialized in Ibero-American culture. It is a non-university research centre and is dedicated to enhancing the dialogue between Germany and Latin America, Spain and Portugal.

The Institute’s 800.000-volume library specialises mainly in earth sciences, economics, social sciences, ethnology, pre-Columbian culture and history, linguistics and literature.

A commission made up of seven experts advises the Institute on questions concerning research, medium and long-term projects and critical evaluation of research.
